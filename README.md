# division-by-zero-fantheory

**FIRST DISCLAIMER** (Preface)

To begin, I shall share that this is not a formal academic paper. If I were to ascribe a kind of name or purpose to this paper, I would describe this paper as being a mathematical “fan fiction”. As unflattering as that label may be, that is the truth of the matter. I, the author, am no real accredited mathematician or academic. I am only an amateur, and my work is simply just that: an interest in a topic put into writing. My work is written to be only conceptual and speculative, without possessing the expected mathematical expertise and rigor that would be seen in an actual, proper academic work - it is a work written for fun, and it began as a hobby. I say this not to discredit myself, but rather to ground myself, as I am aware of my shortcomings. Consider this revelation as being a warning of what is to come, as to my potential readers, it is best to take everything written within this paper with a grain of salt. Expect errors and naive misunderstandings of certain mathematical concepts to be present in this work. However, despite this reality, I have still decided to have my work reach completion. I have faith that my work may have some merit, and potentially may usher in some new ideas which may advance the discipline of mathematics as a whole. It is a dream of mine to have my work get elevated to levels I could never possibly achieve myself.

**SECONDARY DISCLAIMER** (Preface part 2, amateur creation of this theory)

In this paper, the way in which we present our ideas regarding "new" nonstandard analysis and division by zero is through the invention of a number system which is inspired by Abraham Robinson's hyper-real numbers. Although discussion of the hyper-reals and my invented number system shall not be done here, what should be importantly noted, is that this paper does not cover nor really discusses model theory - the branch of mathematics that is, in a sense, required for building a number system (Source: Greg Cherlin, Joram Hirschfeld Ultrafilters and Ultraproducts in Non-Standard Analysis). As a result, many of the standard tools and definitions associated with the construction of the hyper-reals in nonstandard analysis — such as ultrapowers, filters, ultrafilters, the compactness theorem, the transfer principle, and other model-theoretic ideas are **not** explicitly used in constructing this number system and do not appear in this work. This omission is not meant to challenge the validity of these tools, as the reality is the contrary - this theory isn't _beyond_ these tools, but rather, is _behind_ these tools, as these model-theoretic concepts remain outside of my current level of expertise. This is an amateur creation, after all, not an academic one, so this invented number system is not constructed in a rigorous axiomatic manner: instead, naive assertions are made, and we decree that for the most part, it functions similarly to the hyper-reals, albeit with certain modifications which allow for new ideas to emerge. Because of this, I ask the reader to approach this work with appropriate skepticism. Despite these limitations, if you find these ideas worth exploring, (and you are a skilled model theorist or mathematician) I invite you to contact me (via email: emmettlane2004@gmail.com) to discuss how this number system might be further formalized. 

To these extents, I ask you, the reader, to read this work and to pace yourself. It is very long, so it is best read a chapter at a time rather than all at once. With all that being said, I hope you enjoy reading my work :) !
